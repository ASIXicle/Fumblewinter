Fumblewinter.

Poems written while traveling in Italy. By H.M.

11. excerpts 3

The old men in Gerace do not sit often and whole days long I've seen them milling over this old gravel this pack of rocks resting as always tender Being fed and a bit of wine and time, time is comfortable as it walks the Borgo and considers pausing among the cats and garbage and fallen down churches those men turn with three steps like animated tripods and climb back to the top.
