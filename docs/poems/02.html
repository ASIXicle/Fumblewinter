Fumblewinter.

Poems written while traveling in Italy. By H.M.

2. Tutti Saluti

You are better for it so then won't you miserably fashion words in a manner to call the chaps diamonds or forge a new opinion of yourself. No. Instead crease the daylight with the taunt of hello grimaced from a mouth begging the pardon of propriety Your convivial affection no matter the alarming falsity or the obscenity of all these day-times you've maligned been fond to invite then to entertain. Buckle the shade of mid-day with a groan of agreement guttered from the low of your throat coerced from a nod to assuage doubt. No men mattered more to me or less to my memory than those I lilted through their loss with drink and handshakes and solemn oaths to never make a mistake again.
