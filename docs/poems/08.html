Fumblewinter.

Poems written while traveling in Italy. By H.M.

8. excerpts 2

On my skin a bit of the regular shame scent credent yet impaired, imparted to remind my shallow, shallow self of what is acquired by a hand badgered and dumb still swollen from the rush of swordplay still frozen from the frost of Virginian winter still firm from the choke of my enemy still smooth from the bath bore by my widow still vermin that spurns the offered shield the offered cloak the offered bowl the offered cane the offered furnace the offered veil. That which were and that which is and that which will be has always been for me and always will Still I love this dread of nothing for all of the things I have been given.
