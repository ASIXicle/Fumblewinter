Fumblewinter.

Poems written while traveling in Italy. By H.M.

7. end excerpts.

That word they use for laughter is so peculiar in its similarity to my word for to tear asunder. Brought out plain in her eyelids and at their edge, their remarkable edge the famous affinity of love rendered in a curl indiscernible without sound. Might the lowering of clouds to the earth compound air and leaves forge and heavy the uninterested soil that sinks and rises and salves the wounded bent all at once to broker with the dust for one more day year and minute with her loveliness.
