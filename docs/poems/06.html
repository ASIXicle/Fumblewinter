Fumblewinter.

Poems written while traveling in Italy. By H.M.

6. The Sodom Problem

There is something of pigfilth to it all as if the town itself were acrid, ammoniated, offensive to an old god who has contrived to punish all here in the old-fashioned manner of annoyances. They all still keep on though, don't they regardless of the trifles though I think I prefer to respect the likes of Paestum a community that rejected it's curses. As would I, except that I am not unsure why, except that perhaps I believe I've something to learn from them, uncertain if they are even my kin.
